About

Problem Statement

The fact checking tools of today are either too niche or bothersome to tackle this misinformation epidemic. Despite the vastly popular concern regarding digital inaccuracy, social media users frequently propagate misinformation because existing verification methods are inconvenient and require active user intervention.

Problem Characteristics

Inconvenience and Disconnected Verification

Fact-checking often requires manual searching outside the platform. Users simply don't verify information because it takes too much effort.

Lack of Credibility Awareness

Users receive no real-time credibility signals while browsing. Without active warnings, credibility is often assumed by default.

No Motivation to Verify

Platforms and algorithms encourage instant sharing with one click, while verifying claims may take minutes. Rationally, users choose not to spend this time and effort.

Lack of Universal Tool

Many existing verification tools are platform-specific and not unified. For example: Community notes on X, NewsGuard for news sites, L.L.M.'s when manually provided context.

Algorithms Reinforce Bias

User’s algorithms feed them content they inherently agree with which makes misinformation easier to spread.

Solution Statement

RedFlag is a cross-platform fact checking (RedFlag) and bias indicator (BaisStar) running behind the scenes to tackle misinformation. When users are consuming the news any inaccuracies will be marked instantly with credible sources upon request, to prevent the unknowing spread of misinformation in today's information age. Furthermore on politically divisive issues RedFlag will star the content and offer different viewpoints from the left, right, and center at the users choosing.

Solution Characteristics

Convenience and Verification

Fact-checking is quick and painless, requiring little input from the user.

Awareness of Credibility

Users are actively informed of the credibility of sources they view while they’re browsing.

Easily Accessible, Unprompted and Instant Verification

Users are warned and informed before sharing potentially false claims, discouraging impulsive sharing.

One Universal Tool

A general tool for uncovering misinformation, no matter where the user browses.

Sharing different points of view

Users are provided with a multitude of sources leaning in different areas of the political spectrum.

Design Principles

RedFlag is built on principles that prioritize user autonomy and unbiased information.

Unbiased Political Alignment

RedFlag supports decision-making without dictating it. It does not act as a final authority on truth but provides evidence-based evaluation from multiple sources and perspectives on disputed topics.

Comparison & Context

Optional context is provided upon the user's request. It highlights questionable information immediately as it is presented on screen.

Uninterrupted Flow

Pop-ups interrupt the user's browsing experience. RedFlag uses highlighting text, which is significantly less intrusive, maintaining the user's natural browsing flow.

Who Does This Affect?

Teens & Young Adults

85% of young people now get their news primarily from social media.

Older Adults (55+)

On Twitter/X, 80% of fake news was shared by users over age 50.

Everyday Users

Everyday users of the internet are our audience. RedFlag helps them quickly discern misinformation they come across on the web. Customers may include any companies or non-profits interested in combatting misinformation.
