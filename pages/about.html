About



Problem Statement

The fact checking tools of today are either too niche or bothersome to tackle this misinformation epidemic.


Problem Characteristics

Inconvenience and Disconnected Verification

Fact-checking often requires manual searching outside the platform.

Lack of Credibility Awareness

Users receive no real-time credibility signals while browsing.

No Motivation to Verify

Platforms and algorithms encourage instant sharing with one click, while verifying claims may take minutes. Rationally, users choose not to spend this time and effort.

Lack of Universal Tool

Many existing verification tools are platform-specific and not unified. For example:

Community notes on X, NewsGuard for news sites, LLMs when manually provided context.

Algorithms Reinforce Bias

User’s algorithms feed them content they inherently agree with which makes misinformation easier to spread.


Solution Statement

RedFlag is a cross-platform fact checking (RedFlag) and bias indicator (BaisStar) running behind the scenes to tackle misinformation. When users are consuming the news any inaccuracies will be marked instantly with credible sources upon request, to prevent the unknowing spread of misinformation in today's information age. Furthermore on politically divisive issues RedFlag will star the content and offer different viewpoints from the left, right, and center at the users choosing.


Solution Characteristics

Convenience and Verification

Fact-checking is quick and painless, requiring little input from the user.

Awareness of Credibility

Users are actively informed of the credibility of sources they view while they’re browsing.

Easily Accessible, Unprompted and Instant Verification

Users are warned and informed before sharing potentially false claims, discouraging impulsive sharing.

One Universal Tool

A general tool for uncovering misinformation, no matter where the user browses.

Sharing different points of view

Users are provided with a multitude of sources leaning in different areas of the political spectrum.


Customers, End-users, and Stakeholders

Everyday users of the internet will be our audience or end-users for this project. It will help them quickly discern misinformation they come across on the web. Customers may include any companies or non-profits interested in combatting misinformation. Stakeholders will include the customers and users in the interest of keeping the project healthy and creating transparency.
